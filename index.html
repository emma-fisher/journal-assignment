Heavenly Father Has A Plan For Us

The Atonement is part of God's Plan to bring His children back to Him.

As the firstborn of our Heavenly Father, Christ had the responsibility to save His family, which are all the children of God.

Christ was perfect from the very beginning.

He was willing to die to save us from our sins, and to be resurrected to save us from the awful grip of death.

Without the Atonement, our bodies would be dead forever and our spirits would become angels of Satan.

Without the Atonement, our only choice would be to choose captivity and death, so our agency would be void.

The Fall was an essential part of God’s plan for His children.

While in the garden, Adam and Eve had no knowledge or understanding of good versus evil nor were they able to bear children.

    The elements required for agency are:
  • The law defining which choice is right
  • Opposition providing competing interests
  • Knowledge of the choices
  • The power to choose

While in the garden, Adam and Eve had no knowledge or understanding of good versus evil nor were they able to bear children.

    The commands Heavenly Father gave to Adam and Eve when they lived in the Garden of Eden were to:
  • Multiply and replenish, or be fruitful which means to bear children
  • Leave their Father and Mother and cleave unto their spouse
  • Not eat of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil or they would experience death

Adam and Eve couldn’t fully obey God while in the garden, so they needed to transgress the command of God in order to obey His other commandments.









Without the Fall, the rest of mankind would not exist in this mortal world and would never have the chance to prove themselves worthy of eternal life.

Covenants bring us closer to Christ and strengthen us to endure to the end.

The Great Apostasy was caused in part by members of Christ’s church not keeping their covenants and being unfaithful.

Making Covenants with God brings us closer to Him and helps us have a better view of God’s plan for us.

We access the power of God and participate in covenants through the Priesthood, which was restored again in this dispensation to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery.







Without the Priesthood, we are cut off from the Atonement of Jesus Christ.

    The Saving Ordinances, which are the ordinances required for eternal life in which we covenant with God, are:
  • Baptism
  • The Gift of the Holy Ghost
  • Receiving initiatories and endowments
  • Being sealed to your spouse
  • Performing ordinances for the dead
  • Receiving the Priesthood (for men)

Spiritual Gifts are given to us to help us return to Heavenly Father.

Every child of God is given spiritual gifts to help them develop attributes of Christ.

In order to grow our gifts, we need to bless the live of others with them. This in turn blesses us.


We can pray to discover, develop, and receive
spiritual gifts, as our Heavenly Father is always
willing to bless us when we ask Him in faith.

Scriptures help us understand our past, present, and what we must do to attain the future we desire.

The Book of Mormon is the truest book on Earth, although it is not perfect.










The language in which scripture is written is imperfect, but rather than focusing on its imperfections, we need to focus on what we can learn from them.
